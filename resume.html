Thanks for stopping by!
I am currently a sophomore at UCLA progressing towards a degree in Computer Engineering, and I am working at the UCLA School of Law’s IT Department to supplement my education in computer fundamentals.
All my life, I have been fascinated by technology. As a toddler, examining how a CD player worked by jamming multiple CDs into it; in middle school, opening up my Playstation 3 (voiding the warranty) to see how it worked; and in high school, playing with Arduinos to make useless boxes and LED arrays.
It was not long before my curiosity brought me to quantum computing. My passion for hands-on technical work and discovering the unknown is what brought me to this point in my life’s journey. My dream is to make quantum computers more practical for everyday use to give humanity the tools they need for a better tomorrow.
To get there, I aspire to enter a Master’s program in Quantum Computing and gain in-field experience along the way.
